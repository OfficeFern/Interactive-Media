Pick'em Ups

A social app that allows users to create and join pick-upgames of various sports

by Trey Heavner

The Problem

One of the issues teens face when they move away from their childhood homes is making new friends. It can be intimidating to try and figure out where to or how to meet new people without being uncomfortable.

So I was looking for a way to make it easier for these teens to connect with others that have similar interests or that just want to meet new people.

However, I didn't want to just create a social website like Facebook, but something people could use for simply connecting

Design Issues In Research

When I was conducting research for this app the number one issue people would bring up was privacy. "What if someone has predatory intentions for younger teens?" one parent said. Or "what if there is a group of kids that like to bully one specific user when he joins in their game?" another asked.

These were my hardest issues to deal with as I had to come up with something that wouldn't take away from the freedom the app gives like parental permissions before a user joins a game and so on. So in response to those concerns there would be a rating system put in place that increased as a user would play games. After the creator of a game has confirmed that the game has concluded a counter would be increasedfor all players involved to tell how many games the user has participated in. This would mean that players will be discouraged to join games that have low rating users as this is a warning.

Another measure in place for these concerns was the communities tab. Now communities are a collection of users that have common interests when it comes to the type game they like or way a game is played. For example there could be a basketball community that is focused on competitive 1 on 1s while another is based around playing horse.

The Plan
